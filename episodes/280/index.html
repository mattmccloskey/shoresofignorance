Turtles All the Way Down 🐢

Episode 280 · May 21, 2026 · 88 min

🎧 Listen on SoundCloud

About This Episode

Matt and Michael wrestle with one of the oldest questions in philosophy. Why does accepting objective meaning make life harder, not easier?

Key Quotes

"Nihilism is a retreat from meaning. It's not so much a denial of it. It's like RSVPing no to an invitation. You're not saying that the party doesn't exist. You're just saying, I don't want to go."

— Matt, 5:40

"Only people who have tried very hard to be good understand anything about badness."

— C.S. Lewis, quoted by Matt, 9:46

"The truth doesn't need to be defended. My ability or inability to justify the truth doesn't matter to the truth."

— Matt, 31:57

"The only way to be a good person is to stop worrying about being a good person and worry about something that is worth being caught up in."

— Matt, 61:38

"Love keeps no records."

— Matt, 63:07

"Who am I? As I'm the son of God."

— Matt, 72:05

  • Carl Trueman, The Rise and Triumph of the Modern Self — Referenced in an interview on Socrates in the City. Trueman's thesis: pre-modern people defined themselves by externalities (family, land, vocation), while modern people define themselves by internal choices and preferences. - Link ↗
  • Sam Harris — Referenced as someone who has "installed his own wireless dog collar" — refusing to engage with questions that would challenge his materialist worldview. Matt compares him to a dog with an invisible fence who stops dead at arbitrary boundaries.
  • Jordan Peterson — Referenced as someone who invites people to "come play" beyond their boundaries, contrasted with Harris who stays within his fenced plot.
  • Wesley Huff — Referenced for his line on Joe Rogan: "Give me one miracle and I'll explain everything to you." Either the Big Bang or the virgin birth — pick your miracle.
  • Albert Camus — Referenced for his idea that suicide is the ultimate expression of free will, and that "defiance against meaninglessness" was a viable posture.
  • The Lion King (1994) — Matt uses Simba's arc as a metaphor for nihilism: naivety → catastrophe → "Hakuna Matata" (nihilism) → wisdom/return.
  • Socrates in the City — YouTube channel featuring interviews with intellectuals. The Carl Trueman interview was watched by Matt and his mom. - Link ↗
  • Thanos — Used as a thought experiment: if someone with arbitrary power decides your social contract is wrong, what do you do? You either accept it or fight, and fighting requires objective justification.
