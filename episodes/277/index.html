Sex Dolls, Vinyl Records, and AI — The Search for What's Real

Episode 277 · April 23, 2026 · 84 min

🎧 Listen on SoundCloud

About This Episode

Matt and Michael start with death. A healthy forty-six-year-old drops dead, and the conversation turns to what matters when time is short and kids still need raising. Then things get interesting. They dive deep into AI and authenticity, asking what is real and what is most real. Matt predicts a backlash against smartphones and synthetic content, while Michael pushes back with excitement about AI's potential, and they land somewhere in the tension between them. Billy Corgan refuses to touch AI and calls it demonic. Dante puts flattery in the lowest level of hell. Matt connects the dots and argues that if everything becomes fake, we lose the incentive to engage at all. They explore education, relationships, and the sexualization of culture, noting that we live in a highly sexualized society with less sex than ever, where the icon replaces the idol and the idol hollows itself out. Along the way they touch on vinyl records, breast milk, Good Will Hunting, The Matrix, Tom Petty live, and Leonard Cohen versus Jeff Buckley, all circling the same questions. What is a song? What is intimacy? What is truth?
